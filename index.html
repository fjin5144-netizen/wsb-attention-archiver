SKEW = the premium the options market "crash-style declines" pays for crash insurance; long-run center is about 120, >=140 means crowded tail-hedge demand. It measures the price of panic insurance; its predictive power for forward returns has always been weak - treat it as an environment gauge, not a timing signal. GEX/DIX come from the SqueezeMetrics official free history file (refreshed daily by the archiver; if the official source dies it falls back to this site's self-computed naive GEX). Positive GEX = hedging flows dampen volatility; negative = they amplify it, fragile regime. DIX is dark-pool buying intensity (0-1); per its authors' historical research, >=0.45 has typically preceded market strength while <0.40 weakness - their statistical observation, not this site's endorsement. Data cited for personal research with attribution; environment gauges only, not investment advice.
